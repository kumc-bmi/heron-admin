HERON Research Data Repository

HERON (Healthcare Enterprise Repository for Ontological Narration) is a search discovery tool that allows KUMC faculty members to search de-identified data from various hospital and medical center sources. By combining the various data sources, researchers can look at the data in new ways not available when viewing data in one source at a time. Data sources include but are not limited to:

  • Cardiovascular Registry
  • Cystic Fibrosis Registry
  • Epic/O2 (the hospital electronic medical record)
  • IDX (the clinical billing system)
  • KU Hospital Cancer Registry
  • KU Biospecimen Repository
  • REDCap (selected projects)
  • Social Security Death Index
  • Trauma Registry
  • Vizient (Quality Measure Data - formerly University HealthSystem Consortium)

Reasons to use HERON

  1. Search HERON to find the number of KUMC patients who meet inclusion/exclusion criteria
    • Useful for grant applications and feasibility counts
  2. Request de-identified datasets
    • No IRB approval needed
  3. Request identified datasets
    • Will require IRB approval or QI letter of determination from the IRB
  4. Receive data on patients in a research study
    • Please contact heron-admin@kumc.edu to learn how to get data elements on a specific list of MRNs
  5. Receive contact information for patients in FRONTIERS registry who are eligible for your study

Accessing HERON

Check mark(s) missing in the upper right corner under "Login Credentials" indicate a missing requirement. The requirements to access HERON are as follows:

  1. You are a qualified KUMC faculty member or sponsored by a KUMC faculty member.
  2. You are up-to-date on your Human Subjects Training.
  3. You have signed the system access agreement.

HERON Sponsorship Process

Only faculty members may submit a sponsorship request.

  1. Faculty members should visit HERON using the KUMC network or using a KUMC VPN connection
  2. Follow the "HERON Sponsorship Request" under Investigator Resources
  3. Enter the id(s) for sponsoree(s)
  4. Complete the following form and click submit

Sponsorship requests take approximately one week to be approved. Once approved, users can begin searching HERON. More detailed instructions are found on our Frontiers Training Pages.

HERON Data Request Process

After completing a HERON query, users may request identified or de-identified data. Identified data requires IRB approval or a QI letter of determination from the IRB. Faculty or non-faculty members may create the query. However, only faculty members may submit a data request.

  1. Faculty members should visit HERON using the KUMC network or using a KUMC VPN connection
  2. Follow the "HERON Data Request" under Investigator Resources
  3. Enter the id(s) for all individuals needing access to the HERON data
  4. Add all individuals and click "done choosing people"
  5. Complete the following form and click submit

Data requests take approximately two weeks for approval and the release of data to the study team. All individuals needing access to the data must sign the data use agreement prior to release of data. More detailed instructions are found on our Frontiers Training Pages.

More Information

  • KUMC participates in the Greater Plains Collaborative (GPC) which is a network of 12 leading medical centers in 8 states. The network brings together data on over 16 million patients. Investigators from KUMC are able to request data from participating sites.
  • Patient recruitment is possible through the Frontiers and Pioneers Research Registries.
  • HERON data has been cited in several publications.
  • HERON is supported in part by funds from CTSA Award #UL1TR000001 and Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Institute (PCORI) Program Award #CDRN-1306-04631.

Citing HERON

Any publication that results from a project utilizing HERON should cite grant support (CTSA Award # UL1TR000001) and HERON:

  • Expressing observations from electronic medical record flowsheets in an i2b2 based clinical data repository to support research and quality improvement.
    Waitman LR1, Warren JJ, Manos EL, Connolly DW.
    AMIA Annu Symp Proc. 2011;2011:1454-63. Epub 2011 Oct 22.
  • Serving the enterprise and beyond with informatics for integrating biology and the bedside (i2b2).
    Murphy SN, Weber G, Mendis M, Gainer V, Chueh HC, Churchill S, Kohane I.
    J Am Med Inform Assoc. 2010 Mar-Apr;17(2):124-30. doi: 10.1136/jamia.2009.000893.

Publications referencing HERON and the Greater Plains Collaborative (GPC) should also cite the GPC:

  • The Greater Plains Collaborative: a PCORnet Clinical Research Data Network
    Lemuel R Waitman, Lauren S Aaronson, Prakash M Nadkarni, Daniel W Connolly, James R Campbell
    J Am Med Inform Assoc 2014;21:637-641 doi:10.1136/amiajnl-2014-002756
